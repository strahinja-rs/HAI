# Friction Discernment

## Definition

Friction discernment is the meta-skill of choosing where to apply friction in one's life and where to eliminate it. In the age of AI, we will increasingly be able to remove all cognitive friction. This skill has two faces: preventing atrophy on one hand, and choosing a developmental direction on the other.

**Choosing our own friction is choosing our own development.**

## Why This Is New

Every major tool has eliminated some friction and introduced this question implicitly. Plato (in *Phaedrus*) argued that writing would atrophy memory — and he was right; it did. But historically, some friction has always remained imposed. You couldn't choose *all* your frictions.

AGI-level AI is different because it targets *cognitive* friction specifically — the kind that shapes thinking, judgment, and identity. When cognitive friction becomes optional across the board, the choice of where to keep it becomes a defining life skill.

## The Gym Analogy

We already do this with physical friction. No one *needs* to run or lift weights — machines and vehicles eliminated that necessity. But we choose physical friction because we understand it develops us. The same logic now applies to cognitive friction: writing when you could dictate, reasoning when you could ask, remembering when you could look up.

## Key Aspects

- Not just a UX design principle (cognitive forcing functions) — it's a life skill, a stance toward one's own development
- Operates at individual, organizational, and societal levels
- Requires self-knowledge: you must know where you want to develop to choose friction wisely
- Creates a dependency on [[Intent-Expression-Atrophy]]: if you've already atrophied in knowing what you want, you can't choose friction well

- How do you choose friction wisely when you don't yet know what you'll need? (The bootstrapping problem)
